Our main methods

We use qualitative and quantitative research methods to identify bottlenecks, usability issues, and missed opportunities.

Discovery

Validate your assumptions with exploratory research before diving into the design phase. Discover the key features your users actually want and get feedback before you launch.

Usability testing

Test your product on real users and gain actionable insights into their behavior. Uncover usability issues, validate design decisions, and improve user satisfaction.

User interviews

Deep dive into user needs, motivations, and pain points through one-on-one conversations. Understand context, behaviors, and expectations to inform design decisions and product strategy.

UX audit

Improve your product's performance to align with business goals and create a more satisfying user experience. Our experts can quickly pinpoint high-impact areas for improvement, catch underlying issues, and help enhance overall efficiency.

Quantitative research

Measure user behavior at scale through surveys, analytics, and data analysis. Identify patterns, validate hypotheses, and make data-driven decisions with statistical confidence.

AI-based research

Leverage artificial intelligence to accelerate research analysis, identify patterns in large datasets, and generate insights faster. Combine AI tools with human expertise for comprehensive understanding.

Quick guide

Why invest in UX research instead of improving the design directly?

+
-

UX research reveals how people actually interact with your product – what they understand, where they hesitate, and what causes friction. When design decisions are based on real behaviour instead of assumptions, you reduce rework and achieve more predictable outcomes.

What problems can UX research help us solve?

+
-

Research uncovers the gaps between your intended experience and your users' reality. It highlights where people get stuck, why they drop off, and what they expect but don't find. This clarity makes it easier to prioritise improvements and remove friction.

How long does UX research take?

+
-

Timelines depend on depth: quick audit 1–2 weeks, focused research 2–4 weeks, full discovery 4–8 weeks. We share insights as we uncover them, so you gain clarity early rather than waiting until the end.

Can UX research be done remotely?

+
-

Yes. Remote research is efficient, reliable, and allows us to reach a broader audience. It enables natural behaviour, faster scheduling, and full session recordings.
